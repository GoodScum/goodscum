I remember slicing fresh apricots and being happy; to wake up before you or even the sun and boil water for our coffee; to rip fistfuls of alfalfa from the earth just shy of the base for the turkey's breakfast. I did this for the farm, of course, but really: I did this for you.

Those bright, slow days of dipping a chainsaw into cord after cord after cord of hard wood in preparation for the winter, and taking turns with the axe - both of us learning to trust gravity and each other and ourselves to get the job done and done well. Bathing together in the shallow creek at the edge of the acre afterwards, sudsy halos spreading out from where our bare thighs disappeared into the vague murk of cold water where we stood stark naked and in love. I did this and many other things for you, but most notably, most nobly,

I grew. All for you.
