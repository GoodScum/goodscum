How can I say this in a way that doesn't make you lurch towards pity: Three. That's how many times I didn't die by my own hand. Third time's the charm, they say. To which I respond, How many third times before the charm kicks in? Whatever. My point is that I am barely anything, after that. I'm living in the afterlife, now. This very moment. A ghost of the person who wanted to die. Grateful survivor, resilient continuer, lucky to be living, yada yada. I'm transparent. Paper thin. Opacity slider stuck at 33%. Your hand would pass through if you tried to press it to my heart. Third, second, first. Each a door opening into a concrete room with a single door ahead. The door behind seals shut so flush as to vanish completely. Every threshold crossed is a dark wish. Every doorknob leads to that Doorless place. Turn the handle and push. The first time is an accident. The second is a test. The third, a deathly breathlessness you won't live to regret
