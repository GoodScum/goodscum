**Oven:** 350°F  
**Prep Time:** 10-20min
**Cook Time:** 45–55 min, 15–20min rest

## Ingredients

**Batter**
- 1 stick (8 Tbsp) unsalted butter
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- ¾ cup granulated sugar
- 2 tsp baking powder
- ½ tsp cinnamon
- ¼ tsp kosher salt
- 1 cup whole milk
- 1 tsp vanilla extract

**Berries**
- 2 cups frozen blueberries, undefrosted
- ¼ cup granulated sugar
- 1 Tbsp lemon juice
- Pinch of salt

**Top**
- 1 Tbsp granulated sugar

---

## Method

Brown the butter in a saucepan over medium heat, stirring constantly until the milk solids drop and turn amber and the smell goes toasted-hazelnut. Pour immediately into the cast iron — brown flecks and all. Set the pan aside.

Toss the frozen blueberries with the lemon juice, ¼ cup sugar, and a pinch of salt. Set aside.

Whisk flour, ¾ cup sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt in a bowl. Add the milk and vanilla. Whisk to a smooth pancake-like batter.

Pour the batter over the browned butter in the cast iron. Do not stir.

Scatter the blueberries and all their accumulated juice across the top of the batter. Do not stir.

Sprinkle the tablespoon of sugar over everything.

Bake 45–55 minutes until the top is golden brown. The bottom will be dark and caramelized.

Rest 15–20 min because structure needs to set and the berry juices need to thicken.

---

## Notes

- I forgot to add the sugar to the batter, panicked, sprinkled a bunch of sugar on top (less than half a cup) and it was still delicious. If you were to add all the sugar to the berries instead I think it'd still work. 
- Underbake by 3 minutes for a wetter center while the crust stays crackly, resting heat helps consolidate the berry/batter layers.
