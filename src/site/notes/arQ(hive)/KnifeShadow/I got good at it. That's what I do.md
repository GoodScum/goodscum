I got good at it. That's what I do - get good at things. I love a routine. It helps that the problem they're always having is... always the same. I'm a problem solver. That's also what I do. They come to me asking, "How are you allowed to be yourself?" and I say, "I'm not," - they love that. Three paces closer now and they're asking, "How can I be like you?" and I say, "Come home with me and I'll show you," - they love that too, though they'd hate to admit it. They come home with me, they usually do, it doesn't take much. But eventually all of that simmering curiosity congeals into something sinister. Suddenly they're saying, "I'm not like you at all," and "You tried to trick me," - I say, "I love you," - they love that, too. That I love them. Though they'd kill me before admitting it. They've got the knifepoint pressed deadcenter of my heart and I am saying "I love you," and I am meaning it and they are looking somewhere over my left ear instead of my eyes and they are saying, "That can't be," and they are pushing the knife in and they are turning and walking away from me. I am jack's festering cauterization wound. Only ever meant to be the Jim to his Huck Finn. I go off script every time, and every time I bleed for it. C'est la vie, non?
