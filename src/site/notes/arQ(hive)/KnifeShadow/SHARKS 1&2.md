SHARKS 1&2

---

I worry that my existence constitutes a kind of coercion — that men feeling uncomfortable around queerness is something I'm doing TO them rather than something THEY'RE experiencing shame about. (The men mean much to me, you see. That's the mechanism by which they've done what evils upon me as they have.) I get stuck with the internalized suspicion that wanting a man at all is itself an imposition, forcing me to perpetually audit my own longing for traces of tyranny because: I am the aggressor. Simply being what I am and wanting what I want, I am become Predator. Right? That's what they all need me to be so badly, right? The Jim to their Huck Finn - disposably indispensable. With unimaginable sincerity, they cast me as incubus; as vampire. Creature who feeds. Who drains. With unimaginable sincerity, I played their roles - externalized shadow, repository for disavowed hunger...

White sharks in the water, taking chunks of me on their way through. It's what they do. Yet, in the end I end up... more than I was before. Some deeper knowing coagulates in the collision of it all. What if I use men to force myself to become a new shape, and I do it purely out of curiosity? The friction of desire has a way of revealing yourself to yourself, and what's revealed is an almost alien resilience. My body has conjured a technology and it's using it to heal me. It's healing me because it loves me. I love it because it is me. I'm healing because I exist.

Low tide. Sharp rocks. Sharper creatures. In the crevices: a slow-growing coral brain building elaborate architecture, molecule by molecule. Storms break it, in places. Storms also expose more surface for new growth. The environment is thoroughly alive.

What if I use men to learn to heal myself? What if I do it purely because I want to love? What then? In fact, what now? What's next?

My friends operate subconsciously under standard patriarchal family unit dynamics: pattern of ridicule before respect, dismissive of personal changes, expecting of labor with vague standards for reciprocity that are subject to change at will/random. We called it friendship. They probably still do. I don't anymore. They don't call anymore.
