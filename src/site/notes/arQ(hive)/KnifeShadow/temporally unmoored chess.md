Loving them is naught but a game of temporally unmoored chess against the ghosts of their father's fathers' inhumanity. I don't often win, but even winning doesn't staunch 
the bleeding. Loving them wounds me, no matter how I open. No matter the pawn, no matter which square. I am slaughtered either way.
