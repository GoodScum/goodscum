### Men I Fed (On)

Like seasoning a cast iron, I loved them: patiently, with course salt, over years. The process requires a patience the men themselves rarely possess/exhibit. They come hungry, eat what I give them and then leave without ever caring to ask what made the food taste so good. It was that love, of course - worked slow in small circles, over years. Have a seat. Dinner's on.

---

One man asked me what I dreamt about last night, so I proceeded from that moment to dream of him just in case. Eventually he'd start to dream me back, but it spewed out two shades to the left and warped him forever different.

One night of too  many mushrooms found him all piss-soaked in gibberish and grasping at me from the floor at my feet, tears streaming down his face and whispering that I was the most beautiful man he'd ever seen before recoiling in fear of me in the same moment. He'd call me incubus, eventually. I looked it up even though I already knew what it meant but I looked it up anyway: a male entity that feeds on the sleeping.

I was 20 years old and the man I'd travelled the country with for two years straight and built a home with in a grove of curly willow trees had just handed me a myth for my wanting that'd turn out to take me a decade to outgrow.

I learned his favorite flower and grew it in my heart for him, from seed. When it bloomed, I reached in and pulled my ribcage open just enough for him to "Look! See what I've grown inside myself? I grew it for you,"

This interested him less than it terrified him. And then one day he was gone. Away from me, forever.

---

Whiskey bar, back of house. He walked past me down line and my nervous system made an executive decision my prefrontal cortex would spend the next six years regretting. He smelled like a kitchen fire- the  pheromonal lock for which my body was apparently the corresponding key. Most found it repulsive. The stench. He'd been fired from jobs for it. I buried my face in it every chance I got.

To be clear: this was not some degenerate filth kink, nor was it a degredation of self. Allow me to prove it:

Things got different between us on the third sunrise we sauntered into sans sleep. I filled the tub, he peeled off his clothes. I lay myself in the water and he follows, fitting all six foot four of himself in the space between my legs. His head lulls back against my collarbone. My heart beats against his left shoulder. We are all slow breath and safety in these moments. I hadn't set out to, but I end up bathing the boy, head to toe. Not a word from either of us until I'd scrubbed every inch of him clean. If anything, the kink was one of curiosity. The hygeinic standards of white men were so foreign to me that I needed to know why. A need to understand him  became the need to taste him became the need to keep him. His needs led us both into a closet where he'd keep what we did together every thursday night away from the woman he claimed to love.

If you are still seeing a monster where I should be, you're having trouble reading the room. A man who lets you bathe kiss smell taste know love every part of him every Thursday night for a year, but keeps it all from a woman he claimed monogamy to is the antagonist.

It's easy to miss that, somehow. Everyone misses that, somehow.

---

Did you know they're infamous for this, the white men? The find the queer body — brown, specifically, because the melanin carries an additional charge of forbidden — and they use it to access parts of themselves they've been told to kill off. They climb inside the queer body, use their warmth to thaw out their shadows. When the shadows inevitably frighten them for wearing their worst face, they run. They point into the woods and claim the creature who lives there seduced them, tricked them, tried to eat them alive. Are you getting it now?

I have studied this phenomenon with my whole body for nineteen years. Fucked hundreds, loved a handful and been loved back proper by fewer still. The data is conclusive:

The game is rigged and precedes the table, the men who built the table, the seed that grew the tree. The game precedes me and the men I've talked of here entirely. Some hateful sumbitch died believing himself a hero for hurting anyone different before they'd thought to hurt him at all. That hate  seeped into the floorboards of the house he built, and then every house his descendants built, and so on. From these houses men are born who build silences in situations where an honest sentence would've saved everyone involved. The sentence might look something like:

"You are free, and that scares me. The fear makes me cruel, and the cruelty keeps me sorry, and being sorry keeps me at arms length from what it might mean to just love you instead."

I've waited for them to say it. In their bedrooms sticky and half asleep, on porches of people we don't even know at 3am, barefoot in the creek together stacking cairns... The sentence never comes. They do. They always do. But not the truth. Never the truth. So I say it for them, because it's easy. It doesn't help. Never does. I say it anyway.

Someone's got to.
