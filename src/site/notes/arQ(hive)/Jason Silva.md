**Jason Luis Silva Mishkin** (born February 6, 1982) is a [Venezuelan-American](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venezuelan-American "Venezuelan-American") television personality, [short filmmaker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Short_film "Short film"), [futurist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Futurist "Futurist"), and public speaker. He is known for hosting the [National Geographic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Geographic "National Geographic") documentaries *[Brain Games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brain_Games_\(2011_TV_series\) "Brain Games (2011 TV series)")* and *[Origins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Origins:_The_Journey_of_Humankind "Origins: The Journey of Humankind")*. He has stated that his goal is to use technology to excite people about philosophy and science.[^1] *[The Atlantic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Atlantic "The Atlantic")* describes Silva as "A [Timothy Leary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timothy_Leary "Timothy Leary") of the Viral Video Age".[^2] Silva writes and produces short films, is a former presenter on [Current TV](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Current_TV "Current TV"), and lectures internationally on such topics as creativity, spirituality, technology, and humanity.[^2]

He believes in [transhumanism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transhumanism "Transhumanism") and that biology will eventually "become...an information technology".[^3]

## Early life and education

Silva was born on February 6, 1982 in [Caracas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caracas "Caracas"), [Venezuela](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venezuela "Venezuela").[^2] [^4] His mother, Linda Mishkin, an artist, is [Ashkenazi Jewish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashkenazi_Jew "Ashkenazi Jew"). His father, Luis Manuel Silva and whose name is typically portuguese, [converted](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conversion_to_Judaism "Conversion to Judaism") to Judaism, but, according to Silva, they were secular and lived in a household "more akin to a [Woody Allen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Woody_Allen "Woody Allen") film" with "a lot of humor, a love of art... and theater".[^5] He is also brother to Jordan Silva and Paulina Silva.

As described by Silva in interview, he was inspired by [Charles Baudelaire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Baudelaire "Charles Baudelaire") 's *Hashish House* to host "salons" at his house as a teenager to discuss ideas, and it was there that his obsession with filmmaking and documentation began. As he describes it, video rather than pen became his preferred way to memorialize what, in that time, he saw as "ecstatic moments". In response to criticism that he has received "for being overly expository... the equivalent of a voice-over narrator", it's Silva's expressed view that "it's not enough to feel the experience; it needs to be narrated in real time".[^4]

Silva earned a degree in film and philosophy from the [University of Miami](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Miami "University of Miami").[^2] There, he and [Max Lugavere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_Lugavere "Max Lugavere") produced and starred in a video documentary/performance piece entitled *[Textures of Selfhood](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Textures_of_Selfhood&action=edit&redlink=1 "Textures of Selfhood (page does not exist)").*[^6] The short film is "an experimental film about [hedonism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hedonism "Hedonism") and [spirituality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spirituality "Spirituality") " [^6] —described as being based on Silva's and Lugavere's lives in Miami—and it gained the attention of the [Al Gore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al_Gore "Al Gore") - [Joel Hyatt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joel_Hyatt "Joel Hyatt") station, [Current TV](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Current_TV "Current TV"), who, in Silva's words, were looking for "passionate storytellers".[^7]

## Career

Silva is television personality, filmmaker, [futurist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Futurist "Futurist"), and public speaker; he uses television, online media, and lecture halls to share his perspectives.[^2] For example, in the video *The Mirroring Mind*, Silva "explores human consciousness and the creation of that consciousness through self-reference." [^8] He describes himself as a "wonder junkie" [^1] and as a "performance philosopher",[^9] a term he first heard on a website called *[Space Collective](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Space_Collective&action=edit&redlink=1 "Space Collective (page does not exist)")* by [Rene Daalder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rene_Daalder "Rene Daalder").[^4]

### Public speaking

Silva has spoken at [Google](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google "Google"),[^4] *[The Economist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Economist "The Economist")* Ideas Festival,[^4] the [DLD Digital Life Design Conference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_Life_Design "Digital Life Design") in Munich,[^10] [TEDGlobal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TED_\(conference\) "TED (conference)"),[^11] the [Singularity Summit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singularity_Summit "Singularity Summit"),[^4] the PSFK Conference,[^12] and the [Festival of Dangerous Ideas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Festival_of_Dangerous_Ideas "Festival of Dangerous Ideas").[^10] [^13]

At [TEDGlobal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TEDGlobal "TEDGlobal") in June 2012, Silva premiered a short video entitled "Radical Openness".[^8] [^14] In September 2012, Silva presented his Radical Openness videos at the opening keynote at Microsoft [TechEd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TechEd "TechEd") Australia.[^15] Radical Openness was also featured in his presentation at [La Ciudad de las Ideas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Ciudad_de_las_Ideas "La Ciudad de las Ideas") conference on November 10, 2012.

In September 2012, he presented "We Are the Gods Now" at the Festival of Dangerous Ideas.[^16]

## Television

### Current TV

From 2005 to 2011, Silva was a presenter and producer on [Al Gore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al_Gore "Al Gore") 's cable network Current TV.[^4] [^9] [^17]

### Brain Games

In 2013, Silva and [Apollo Robbins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apollo_Robbins "Apollo Robbins") became the hosts of *[Brain Games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brain_Games_\(National_Geographic\) "Brain Games (National Geographic)")* on the [National Geographic Channel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Geographic_Channel "National Geographic Channel"). The show explores the brain through interactive games that look at [perception](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perception "Perception"), [decision-making](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decision-making "Decision-making"), and patterning, as well as how easily the brain can be fooled.[^18] [^19] Experts in psychology, cognitive science and neuroscience appear on the show, as Silva states, "to make sure we're doing the science right." [^20] The show, which premiered in 2011, received 1.5 million viewers for episodes one and two and set a National Geographic record as the highest rated series launch in that channel's history.[^9] [^21]

> The experience of being tricked or fooled or made aware of a shortcoming makes you curious. It makes you kind of get up from your seat a little bit and be like 'How did that work?'

— Jason Silva [^22]

### Origins: The Journey of Humankind

Released in 2017 as a part of the National Geographic Channel, Silva hosted this short series. *[Origins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Origins:_The_Journey_of_Humankind "Origins: The Journey of Humankind")* explores the very beginning of mankind and rewinds all the way back to the beginning, tracing the innovations that made us modern. The series features 8 episodes of approximately 45 minutes running time each, and as of this date, only a commitment to one season of *Origins* has been reported.[^23]

### Guest appearances

In August 2012, he appeared on *[CBS This Morning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CBS_This_Morning "CBS This Morning")*.[^24] In September 2012, he appeared on Australian [ABC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australian_Broadcasting_Corporation "Australian Broadcasting Corporation") program *[Q&A](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Q%26A_\(Australian_talk_show\) "Q&A (Australian talk show)")*.[^25] He has appeared as a guest on Season 2 of [*StarTalk*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StarTalk_\(2015_TV_series\) "StarTalk (2015 TV series)").[^26] [^27]

## Original internet content

### "Shots of Awe"

In May 2013, Jason began "Shots of Awe", a [YouTube channel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/YouTube_channel "YouTube channel") on the [Discovery Digital Networks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_Digital_Networks "Discovery Digital Networks") [TestTube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TestTube "TestTube"), presenting weekly "micro-documentaries" on creativity, innovation, exponential technology, futurism, metaphysics, existentialism and the human condition.[^28] [Zoltan Istvan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zoltan_Istvan "Zoltan Istvan"), editor for the *[Huffington Post](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huffington_Post "Huffington Post")*, wrote that *Shots of Awe* is a blend of philosophy and art and has been massively popular to the younger generation.[^29]

### Other appearances

In October 2013, Silva appeared on YouTube for [Google](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google "Google") and [NASA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NASA "NASA") 's Quantum Artificial Intelligence Lab.[^30] He has appeared multiple times as a guest on the *[Joe Rogan Experience](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joe_Rogan_Experience "Joe Rogan Experience")* podcast.

In March 2014, he was a guest in an episode of [SourceFed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SourceFed "SourceFed") 's Tabletalk.[^31] In December 2017, he appeared as a guest host on the trivia app [HQ Trivia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HQ_Trivia "HQ Trivia").

### Other accomplishments

Silva has been featured in *[The Atlantic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Atlantic "The Atlantic")*,[^2] *[The Economist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Economist "The Economist")*,[^32] *[Vanity Fair](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanity_Fair_\(magazine\) "Vanity Fair (magazine)")*,[^33] *[Forbes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forbes "Forbes")*,[^34] [^35] [^36] *[Wired](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wired_\(magazine\) "Wired (magazine)")*,[^37] and many others.

In 2011, he became a fellow at the Hybrid Reality Institute, examining the [symbiosis between man and machine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symbiosis_between_man_and_machine "Symbiosis between man and machine").[^9] [^38]

His film *Attention: The Immersive Power of Cinema* [^39] was part of the exhibition 'Kino und der kinamatografische Blick' ('Cinema and the cinematographic gaze'), 20 March - 2 June 2013, at MEWO Kunsthalle in [Memmingen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memmingen "Memmingen") ([Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Germany "Germany")).[^40]

In 2014, Silva was an advisor for National Geographic Channel's Expedition Granted competition in which finalists are chosen based on their project's originality, viability and potential impact on either the local or global community.[^41]

Silva has a chapter giving advice in [Tim Ferriss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tim_Ferriss "Tim Ferriss") ' book [Tools of Titans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tools_of_Titans "Tools of Titans").
