true stories

1

A man in his mid-30's came back to Walmart looking for us.

We'd been panhandling out front, and he thought that our “Broken Time Machine” sign taped to the window of the bus was hilarious. He gave us $40 for gas and invited us to his house. His mother fed us. His father gave us permission to shower.

On every shelf or tabletop, there stood a unique and tacky unicorn figurine.


2

On this night we were parked outside of closed bakery. I sat on the asphalt, back against a tire, and listened to M speak soft Armenian into the phone a few feet away. His parents worried about him.

Just as I fell a little bit more in love, he looked up at me and smiled.

3

Exhausted and confused for getting lost, we decided to stop for the night on a dirt road in farm country. In the morning we made coffee and learned that across the street was an orange orchard. We took turns hopping the fence and filling our shirts with perfectly ripe globes of sunshine.

A bee drowned herself in my coffee; tricked by the honey stuck to the cup. I gently fished the corpse out with a finger, and afterwards I dug a hole in the ditch with my hands.

I wrapped the bee in a small coil of orange peel and placed her into the grave.

I pulled two handfuls of dandelions from the earth, arranged them like a halo around her body, and covered it all with dirt.

4

#4 happened two years ago to the day.

today is my personal holiday, marking eight (8) years past.
