# Garden Design Sampler IV

Three prisms. Same DNA as Hilbert — void dark, clean sans-serif, chromatic aberration — but the prism as literal organizing principle. Light enters whole. The garden splits it. The reader decides what to do with the spectrum.

Headings are pure spectral colors (h1 red, h2 blue, h3 violet). Blockquotes are the recombined state: clean white serif, no shadow, the moment where the reader has done the synthesis. The title itself is a spectral gradient via `background-clip: text`. The reader IS the second prism — reading is the act of recombination. The most conceptually layered: dispersion is the default; clarity is what you earn.

## The prism family

| | **Prism A** | **Prism B** (dispersion) | **Prism C** (recombination) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Aberration on** | links + headings | the list as a whole | all body text |
| **What the color does** | displacement resolves on hover | each entry IS a wavelength | dispersion is the default state |
| **Headings** | white + aberration shadow | white + aberration shadow | pure spectral color (no shadow) |
| **Body text** | grey + aberration | spectral per-item | grey + persistent aberration |
| **Blockquotes** | clean white serif | clean white serif | clean white serif (earned) |
| **Title** | white serif + displacement | white serif + displacement | **gradient text** (spectrum across letters) |
| **List structure** | flat, uniform | spectral gradient (red→violet) | flat, uniform |
| **Conceptual move** | garden refracts the writing | garden has already dispersed | reader recombines the spectrum |
| **Complexity** | simplest | moderate | most layered |
| **Random btn** | refract | refract | refract |
